about us/contact

Into the flickering light of Hackney's semi-submerged worlds, an eponymous taxi takes you on a ride through dreams in search of a very specific Society.

Formed in the early 1600s, the Bruschetta Society were the bastion of faded sensibilities. Led by careful ritual, exquisite taste and selective silence, the Society still appears on occasional nights of meaning, amidst the concrete brutalist of the neighbourhood's increasing contradictions.

Let the cabbie weave you through backstreets populated by the stories of people now too small for history, and you may find them, the satin, the strangeness.
The Bruschetta Society was a 45 minute walking tour through Hackney, that included a fictional taxi journey punctuated by unexpected phone calls, a bruschetta-based ritual on the grounds of a striking council estate and a small street protest based on those of the weavers on the 1800s. This tour combined audio, performance and installation.
